THE DISTINCTION

Those are signatures.

Connections between a drawing and a company. They're part of the brand identity: all the tangible assets a company uses to communicate its identity.

Colors. Fonts. Shapes. Logos.

A good logo can be drawn by anyone in seconds. That means it's memorable, recognizable, and scalable. But it's still just a piece of drawing.

THE FOUNDER-BRAND SPECTRUM

Every founder sits somewhere on this spectrum.

Where you sit changes everything: your website, content, pricing, hiring, exit plan.

Founder Brand

The founder IS the brand. Their name, face, and voice drive everything.

Gary Vaynerchuk, Oprah, Chris Do

SHARED

Both reinforce each other

Company Brand

The company IS the brand. The founder is invisible.

Most B2B, most Fortune 500

Most founders make this decision by accident, defaulting to wherever they're comfortable... which usually means hiding behind the company.

STRATEGIC DECISIONS

This isn't a preference. It's a strategy.

Invest in the founder

  • Early-stage companies
  • Service businesses
  • Thought leadership plays
  • Fundraising (investors back people)

Invest in the company

  • Product companies at scale
  • Preparing for exit or acquisition
  • Multiple founders, no single face
  • Regulated industries

Do both

  • Most cases
  • Make them reinforce each other
  • Founder opens doors, company closes deals

Let one go

  • Founder brand carrying a dead company brand
  • Company outgrew the founder's involvement
  • Pivot or rebrand required

The answer to this question changes everything.

Website

THE FRAMEWORK

The Focus Star

Five dimensions of brand differentiation.

THE FOCUS STAR

Five attributes. One position.

Audience

Who you serve

Purpose

Why you exist

DNA

What you're about

Personality

How you show up

Product

What you offer

The goal is not to differentiate on just one. Find the right combination of all five. Most companies don't have a differentiated product... and that's actually most cases.

01
FOCUS STAR / PRODUCT

WD-40. Q-Tips. Kleenex.

Brands that became the product category itself.

Requires monopoly-level marketing power and legacy. Nearly impossible for startups, especially in services.

If your only differentiation is rational product features (price, speed, customer service), those are table stakes. When someone does the same thing cheaper, there's no loyalty.

02
FOCUS STAR / PERSONALITY

ONE Logistics.

Flashy pink containers and boats. Looks wildly different from every other logistics company.

But nothing behind it. Not great pricing, not special to work with. No purpose. No substance.

Personality alone is not enough. Looking different without being different is a house of cards.

03
FOCUS STAR / DNA

The one thing you're fundamentally about.

Red Bull

DNA = Adrenaline culture.

Every ad has nothing to do with the product. Sports cars, planes, extreme sports. They barely mention the drink. The product is almost irrelevant.

Starbucks

DNA = Convenience.

App design, name-calling system, order-ready efficiency. Not about the coffee (which isn't that good). Just convenience everywhere.

Should be expressible in one word. Companies with 8-10 values on their website are saying nothing.

04
FOCUS STAR / PURPOSE

TOMS.

One-for-one: buy a pair, give a pair to a child in need.

Product isn't differentiated. Design isn't great. No clear DNA.

But people joined a movement, not a product company.

Their slogan isn't a tagline. It's an identity: "One for One."

05
FOCUS STAR / AUDIENCE

Rad Coffee.

Small punk rock coffee shop in Upland, CA. 10-person capacity, no chairs, always a line.

140K Instagram followers for 3 locations.

Full of obscure punk band posters from the 70s-90s. Fans drive far because it's their place.

THE HALO EFFECT

Credibility in one area makes people assume you're good at everything.

Apple

Computer company makes music players, then phones. It works because brand credibility.

Microsoft

Makes a music player. Nobody wants it. No halo effect outside computers.

Celebrities

Actor plays 3 guitar chords, everyone loses their minds. Athletes get asked about geopolitics.

NARROWING YOUR AUDIENCE

The Triangle.

"We sell drinks"

Competing with everything. Including free water.

"We sell great coffee"

Competing with every coffee shop.

"Coffee for Pokemon fans"

Every fan in 30 miles comes, posts, talks. You grow fast.

Back to Rad Coffee: their tribe is emo/goth/punk. But the halo effect means non-tribe people come too.

APPLYING IT

How the five attributes work together.

FOCUS STAR IN ACTION

Each attribute informs real decisions.

Audience

Brand identity, product adaptation, content targeting, platform choices

DNA

Content filter, UX alignment, delightful details. "Would Red Bull show a guy on a couch? No."

Personality

Visual language, colors, typography, photography style, tone of voice

Purpose

Vision, recruitment, culture, partnerships, pro bono, "why should people join?"

Product

Innovation direction, feature priorities, usability, design decisions

Consistency

Apply everything everywhere. Adapt to mediums, but keep promise, identity, and audience constant.

DELIGHTFUL DETAILS

Apple lets you choose your hold music.

Simple. Cheap to implement. Completely aligned with their UX-first DNA.

Pre-purchase, purchase, post-purchase. Every touchpoint is an opportunity to reinforce the brand. Top to bottom of the funnel.
